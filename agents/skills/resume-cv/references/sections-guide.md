# CV Sections Guide

This guide provides detailed instructions for writing each section of an effective CV/resume. Section order should be tailored to highlight the user's strongest selling points first.

---

## 1. Header

The header is your first impression. Keep it clean and scannable.

### Required Elements
- **Full Name** - Large, prominent (not all caps)
- **Target Role/Title** - What you want to be hired as
- **Email** - Professional address, hyperlinked (`mailto:`)
- **Phone** - With country code for international applications
- **Location** - City, Country (or "Remote" + timezone)

### Optional Elements
- **LinkedIn** - Hyperlinked, use custom URL if available
- **GitHub** - For technical roles, hyperlinked
- **Portfolio/Website** - If relevant to the role
- **Other Links** - Behance, Dribbble, blog, etc.

### Best Practices
- All links must be clickable hyperlinks
- No photos (unless required by country/industry)
- No date of birth, marital status, or other personal details
- Use a modern, readable font

---

## 2. Professional Summary

A 3-4 sentence elevator pitch that answers: "Why should I interview this person?"

### Formula
1. **Who you are** - Years of experience + core expertise
2. **What you do** - Primary skills and domains
3. **What you've achieved** - 1-2 signature accomplishments with metrics
4. **What you want** - The type of role/company you're targeting (optional)

### Guidelines
- Write in first person without pronouns ("Built..." not "I built...")
- Lead with your strongest credential
- Include at least one quantified achievement
- Tailor to each application
- Keep to 50-75 words

---

## 3. Key Achievements

A dedicated section for your most impressive, quantified accomplishments. Use this to front-load impact before the reader reaches your work history.

### Formula: Action + Context + Result
- **Action verb** - What you did (Led, Built, Reduced, Increased)
- **Context** - The situation or challenge
- **Result** - Quantified impact (numbers, percentages, money)

### Guidelines
- 3-5 bullet points maximum
- Each bullet should be 1-2 lines
- Prioritize achievements relevant to target role
- Include metrics in every bullet
- Use varied action verbs

---

## 4. Skills

A scannable overview of your technical and professional capabilities.

### Organization Options
- **By category** - Languages, Frameworks, Tools, Platforms, Methodologies
- **By proficiency** - Expert, Proficient, Familiar
- **By relevance** - Primary skills, Secondary skills

### Guidelines
- Include years of experience for key skills when relevant
- Group related skills together
- Order by relevance to target role
- Avoid rating systems (1-5 stars, percentages)
- Include both hard and soft skills sparingly
- Remove outdated or irrelevant skills

---

## 5. Work Experience

The core of your CV. Tell impact stories, not job descriptions.

### Each Entry Should Include
- **Company name** - Hyperlinked to company website
- **Your title** - As it appeared or a standardized equivalent
- **Dates** - Month/Year to Month/Year (or "Present")
- **Location** - City, Country (or "Remote")
- **Team context** - Team size, your role within it
- **3-5 bullet points** - Achievements, not responsibilities

### Bullet Formula: CAR (Challenge, Action, Result)
1. What problem or challenge existed
2. What action you took
3. What measurable result occurred

### Guidelines
- Focus on impact, not duties
- Start each bullet with a strong action verb
- Include metrics wherever possible
- Mention technologies and tools used
- Link to projects, articles, or demos where applicable
- Use past tense for previous roles, present for current

### What to Avoid
- Copy-pasting job descriptions
- Listing every task you ever did
- Using weak verbs (helped, assisted, worked on)
- Including irrelevant roles (unless filling gaps)

---

## 6. Projects / Open Source / Hackathons

Demonstrates initiative, passion, and real-world skills outside of employment.

### For Each Project Include
- **Project name** - Hyperlinked to repo or demo
- **Your role** - Creator, contributor, team member
- **Brief description** - What it does in one sentence
- **Technologies used** - Languages, frameworks, tools
- **Recognition** - Downloads, stars, awards, press

### Guidelines
- Lead with most impressive projects
- Include both personal and collaborative work
- Highlight contributions to well-known open source projects
- Mention hackathon wins or placements
- Link to live demos when available

---

## 7. Education & Certifications

Academic credentials and professional certifications.

### Education Format
- **Degree** - Full name (e.g., Bachelor of Science in Computer Science)
- **Institution** - School name, hyperlinked
- **Graduation date** - Year only (or expected date)
- **Notable achievements** - GPA if strong, honors, relevant coursework

### Certifications Format
- **Certification name** - Full official name
- **Issuing organization** - AWS, Google, etc.
- **Date obtained** - Year
- **Credential ID** - Hyperlinked to verification if available

### Guidelines
- Recent graduates: Education near top, with more detail
- Experienced professionals: Education near bottom, minimal detail
- Certifications: Prominent if relevant to role (e.g., AWS certs for cloud roles)
- Include relevant coursework only if lacking experience
- Omit high school once you have a degree

---

## 8. Additional Information

Optional section for differentiating details that don't fit elsewhere.

### What to Include
- **Publications** - Papers, articles, blog posts
- **Speaking** - Conferences, meetups, podcasts
- **Awards** - Industry recognition, company awards
- **Community** - Organizer roles, mentorship, volunteering
- **Languages** - Spoken languages with proficiency
- **Interests** - Only if genuinely interesting or conversation-starting

### Guidelines
- Only include if it strengthens your candidacy
- Keep brief - one line per item
- Hyperlink everything that has a URL
- Avoid generic hobbies (reading, travel, music)
- Include only verifiable claims

---

## Section Order Recommendations

The order of sections should highlight your strongest selling points:

**Career Changers / Recent Graduates:**
1. Header
2. Summary
3. Skills
4. Projects
5. Education
6. Work Experience
7. Additional

**Experienced Professionals:**
1. Header
2. Summary
3. Key Achievements
4. Work Experience
5. Skills
6. Education
7. Additional

**Technical Specialists:**
1. Header
2. Summary
3. Skills
4. Key Achievements
5. Work Experience
6. Projects
7. Education

Adjust based on what makes your case strongest for the specific role you're targeting.
